Fun Facts

Flamingos are not pink. They are born grey, their diet of brine shrimp and blue green algae contains a natural pink dye called canthaxanthin that makes their feathers pink.

Hummingbirds are the only known birds that can also fly backwards. They often do this when retreating away from flowers. They are also the smallest bird in the world and heavy as a sheet of paper.

The most deadly animal in the world isn't a shark, tiger or bear. But something supprisely smaller-the mosquito. According to the World Health Organization, 725,000 of people are killed each year from mosquito-borne diseases.

The shortest living animal in the world is the Mayfly. Its entire adult lifespan is just 24hrs! Mayflies start their life underwater and some of them can only survive for just 5 minutes, the lucky ones might manage to survive a few days.

Koalas can sleep for about 18 to 20 hours a day. Koala need more sleep than most animals, because their diet of eucalyptus leaves contain toxins, are very low in nutrition and barely have any sugar, so they take a large amount time to resting and conserving their energy.

Koala!

Swifts spend most of their lives flying in the air, and can fly for almost an entire year, without ever landing. A study showed that over a 10-month period, a swift stopped for just 2 hours.

The Giant Pacific Octopus is the largest octopus in the world and they typically reach about 4.9 meter across. It has 3 hearts, 9 brains and blue blood. They are also able to change their colour and texture to camouflage themselves in a blink of an eye.

A Blue Whales tongue can weigh as much as a car, or a small adult elephant. A Blue Whales tongue can weigh approximately 2.7 tonnes, or 6,000 to 8,000 pounds (2,720 to 3,630 kg).

Large Whales!

Pufferfish can contain a tetrodoxin, a toxin that is up to 1,200 times more deadly than cyanide to humans. There is enough toxin in one pufferfish to kill 30 adult humans, and there is no known antidote.

The fastest land animal in the world is a cheetah. It can reach speeds of up to 120 kilometer per hour. Cheetah also one of few big cats that can't roar, instead they purr like a cat. They also chirp loudly enough to be heard a mile(1.6 km) away.

Cheetah chirp!

The howler monkey is the loudest land animal. Its calls can be heard from 3 miles (5 km) away. At its peak, the howler monkey can produce sounds that reach 140 decibels. That's as noisy as a jet engine, on take off!

Howler roaring

An ostrich legs are so powerful that their kicks can kill a lion. Or a human! Each two-toed foot has a long, sharp claw making them formidable weapons. Animals often end up running away from the ostrich.

Hyena run away

The Japanese Spider Crab has the longest leg span of any arthropod. From the tip of one claw to the other can be as long as almost 4m! Japanese spider crabs have 10 legs stretching out from a central body, giving it its spider like appearance. They live deep in the oceans south of Japan.

Largest crab in the world

Koala's have fingerprints almost identical to human. It is so hard to distinguish between the fingerprints of koalas and humans that even under a microscope it is hard to tell. There are even reported cases of a koala's fingerprints confusing forensics at crime scenes.

White-tailed jackrabbits are the greatest land jumpers, having been recorded leaping an astonishing 6.4 meter vertically. That's higher than 3 average sized men all stood on top of each other. This large species of hare can also run up to 55 kilometer per hour when escaping predators.
